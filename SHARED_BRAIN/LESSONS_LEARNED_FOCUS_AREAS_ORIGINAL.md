## Content

**Primary focus areas**

The lessons learned session isn't just about closing the project; it becomes a strong repository of actionable insights for the next big project. These insights become clear when PMs focus on a few primary areas.

---

### 1. Project objectives and scope

The project team evaluates the overall project to:

- Review if the project objectives and scope were clearly defined and met.
- Understand the scope changes and their impact on the project.
- Compare the outcomes with the objectives.

**Example:** GreenBambs revised the core message of the campaign during the design phase. This led to Ryan's team updating the project objectives, strategy, and deliverables. As a result, the initiate and plan phase of the project required more effort than Ryan had originally planned.

---

### 2. Project planning and execution

The team assesses the project's plan and execution to:

- Analyze the effectiveness of the project planning process.
- Evaluate how well the project plan was executed and adhered to.
- Discuss deviations from the plan and how they were managed.

**Example:** Ryan's project planning was robust. After he experienced scope change in the design phase, he added buffer time during project execution. Project leads appreciated this.

---

### 3. Stakeholder engagement and communication

The team reviews stakeholder involvement to:

- Assess the level of engagement and communication with clients and stakeholders throughout the project.
- Identify what worked well in managing stakeholder expectations and what could be improved.

**Example:** According to Ryan's communication management plan, he was the single point of contact for all communication with GreenBambs. However, as the project progressed, a few leads also started sending updates and deliverables, which caused some confusion among the client's team. The team noted this point for future projects and decided to reevaluate having multiple points of contact.

---

### 4. Team performance and collaboration

The team assesses their teamwork to:

- Evaluate the performance of the project team and their collaboration.
- Identify skill gaps and training needs.
- Discuss team dynamics, motivation, and other issues that arose.

**Example:** Ryan applauded the team's effort in delivering the GreenBambs PR campaign. He also noted that some vendors needed training on their processes.

---

### 5. Risk management

The team analyzes how they addressed the project risks to:

- Review the risk management process, including risk identification, assessment, and mitigation.
- Identify how they handled the unforeseen risks.

**Example:** Ryan's quality lead voiced her concern about incomplete legal documentation provided by GreenBambs that led to a delay in quality approval. The team discussed how to best handle similar challenges in future projects.

---

### 6. Resource management

The team evaluates the project's resources to:

- Assess the allocation and usage of resources, including time, budget, and people.
- Identify how they managed the resource constraints.

**Example:** Managing resources was a major challenge in Ryan's project. The team had to accommodate a delay in the design phase, which required adding more team members during execution. Ryan and his team had to pivot by working with a vendor partner to scale up the team.

---

### 7. Quality management

The team reviews the project's overall quality performance to:

- Review the quality assurance and quality control processes.
- Assess whether the project deliverables met the required quality standards.

**Example:** The vendor team members assigned to the quality assurance team were not familiar with the process. Their initial work did not meet quality standards, so the QA lead had to spend some extra effort to upskill them.

---

### 8. Client and stakeholder satisfaction

The team evaluates the overall project feedback to:

- Assess the client and stakeholders' satisfaction level with the project outcomes.
- Identify areas where expectations were not met.

**Example:** Ryan happily shared that GreenBambs had given five stars to the team. The client was happy with the project's execution but provided feedback on key expectations for future projects, such as maintaining a single point of contact with their PM.

---

### 9. Process improvement

The team discusses the overall process to:

- Identify improvements in processes or methodologies for future projects.
- Capture suggestions for best practices and process enhancements.

**Example:** The Graphic team suggested agile reviews by internal stakeholders of deliverables to save time. The Marketing team proposed adding a "mock" strategy presentation step for new clients.
