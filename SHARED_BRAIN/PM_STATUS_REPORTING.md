# STAKEHOLDER STATUS REPORTING

---

## ORIGINAL TEXT:

**Why report status to stakeholders?**

Think about yourself as a client. If the PM working on your project doesn't give you frequent updates on the project, you might find yourself wondering about the status.

Now think about a project lead. If the PM doesn't inform the lead about the next steps and associated tasks, the lead might not align enough resources in a timely way.

Everyone benefits from status reporting, even the PM!

Here are key reasons why status reporting is so important.

- Builds transparency and trust: Regular updates keep stakeholders informed and builds trust by showing progress and challenges.
- Supports decision making: Stakeholders rely on status reports to make critical decisions regarding resource allocation, risk mitigation, and project direction.
- Manages expectations: Frequent reporting helps to align stakeholders' expectations, reducing the possibility of surprises.
- Identifies risks: Status reports bring attention to emerging risks, ensuring proactive problem solving and management of potential issues.
- Ensures accountability: Regular updates create a sense of accountability by keeping team members and stakeholders aware of responsibilities, progress, and any necessary follow-ups.

The real value of a project status report goes beyond just communication. It also creates a record of the project's history, providing PMs with data to avoid mistakes and bottlenecks in future projects.
