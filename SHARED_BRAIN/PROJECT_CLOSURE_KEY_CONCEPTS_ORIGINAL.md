## Content

**Key concepts**

1. During the close the project phase, project managers provide a closure to the project by reviewing what went well and what can be improved in the next project.

2. PMs perform the following three steps with the team's support to close the project:

   **Step 1:** Complete documentation and archive files
   
   **Step 2:** Conduct a lessons learned session
   
   **Step 3:** Celebrate success and release the team

3. Documenting and archiving project files is important for maintaining a historical record, capturing lessons learned, facilitating knowledge transfer, ensuring compliance, supporting project continuity, and enhancing client satisfaction.

4. During the lessons learned session, PMs and project teams focus on several discussion areas, such as project objectives and scope, stakeholder engagement and communication, team performance and collaboration, and process improvement.

5. To celebrate success and release the team, PMs organize a celebration event, officially release project resources, complete and communicate project and administrative closure, and ensure transition and support.

6. By systematically addressing the three steps, project managers can ensure a thorough project closure, readying the team for future work.
