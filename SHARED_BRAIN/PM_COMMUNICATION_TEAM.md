# COMMUNICATION MANAGEMENT & TEAM SETUP

---

## ORIGINAL TEXT:

**Key concepts**

**1.** To complete the planning part of the initiate and plan a project phase, project managers create communication management and risk management plans and set up a project team.

**2.** Communication management is the process of ensuring clear and effective communication between the project team and stakeholders. With effective communication, PMs can establish appropriate communication channels and share crucial information with the right individuals.

**3.** A communication management plan is a document that identifies how to share important information with the right people at the right time during a project. The main components of a good communication management plan are: Type of communication, Frequency of communication, Expectations for all stakeholders, Details of senders and receivers.

**4.** Risk management is the systematic process of identifying and addressing potential risks that might impact a project. By proactively managing risks, PMs can minimize the impact of the risks and avoid delays.

**5.** The risk management process is a structured step-by-step approach that helps PMs systematically identify, evaluate, and respond to potential risks throughout a project's lifecycle.

**6.** The outcome of a risk management process is the risk management plan, which is a detailed document that defines how the project team addresses risks and establishes processes for risk management throughout the project.

**7.** The risk management process includes five steps: Step 1: Identify risks, Step 2: Assess and prioritize risks, Step 3: Analyze risks quantitatively, Step 4: Plan risk responses, Step 5: Monitor and adapt the approach.

**8.** Based on the project requirements, PMs need to build well-rounded teams. When choosing team members, PMs consider skills and expertise, cost, experience, availability, and dynamics.

**9.** While PMs aim to build strong in-house teams, some projects require external expertise or additional support. In such cases, PMs might outsource and hire external team members.

**10.** To officially start a project, PMs conduct a kickoff meeting with the project team, stakeholders, and client to align them on project goals, scope, and expectations.
