# EXECUTE THE PROJECT PHASE

---

## ORIGINAL TEXT:

**Key concepts**

**1.** The execute the project phase is the longest phase where the project team completes the assigned tasks and collaborates effectively, and PMs manage resources to successfully deliver the project.

**2.** A high-performing team efficiently and consistently uses its varied functional and behavioral skills, proficiency, and experience to achieve the set goals. To ensure that the team is a high-performing team, PMs apply several guidelines, including ensuring alignment with project goals and providing support and feedback.

**3.** A high-performing team has a shared vision and commitment, communicates and collaborates openly, respects each team member, has clear roles and responsibilities and a supportive environment.

**4.** A remote team is a group of geographically dispersed individuals who work flexibly from home or other locations and collaborate and achieve project goals using digital communication tools.

**5.** A remote team setup offers several advantages, including creativity, innovation, and cost efficiency, but also comes with constraints, including communication barriers and challenges in team unity.

**6.** A cross-cultural team is a group of individuals from different cultural backgrounds, working toward a common goal. To help a cross-cultural team collaborate effectively, PMs follow several guidelines, such as clear communication, comprehensive decision making, and flexible work approach.

**7.** PMs set up several meetings to facilitate communication with stakeholders, considering several factors, such as scope, agenda, participants, availability, and duration.

**8.** Based on the project's requirements, PMs schedule different types of meetings, such as status update, stakeholder engagement, and event-related meetings. To effectively conduct team meetings, PMs follow several strategies, such as creating a detailed agenda, limiting attendees, and maintain the start and end time.

**9.** Quality in project management refers to the degree to which a project's deliverables align with the predefined requirements and standards. To effectively manage quality, PMs and project teams follow six steps: Step 1: Establish a quality plan, Step 2: Define quality standards, Step 3: Set success metrics, Step 4: Define quality assurance and control processes, Step 5: Assign roles, Step 6: Monitor quality-related tasks.
